Patient Care Guidelines

At Sree Nandanam Ayurveda Hospital, we are committed to providing safe, ethical, and effective Ayurvedic care rooted in traditional wisdom and modern clinical standards. These guidelines are designed to ensure a smooth, comfortable, and beneficial healing experience for every patient.

Admission & Consultation

  • All patients must undergo a detailed consultation with our Ayurvedic physicians prior to starting treatment.
  • Treatment plans are individualized based on body constitution (Prakriti), disease condition, and medical history.
  • Patients are advised to provide complete and accurate medical information, including previous treatments, medications, and reports.
  • In-patient admission is recommended only when necessary for intensive therapies like Panchakarma.

Treatment Protocols

  • All therapies are prescribed and supervised by qualified Ayurvedic doctors.
  • Duration of treatment may vary (commonly 7–30 days) depending on the condition and response to therapy.
  • Patients must strictly follow the prescribed treatment schedule and instructions given by therapists and doctors.
  • Any discomfort or side effects during treatment must be reported immediately.

Diet & Lifestyle (Pathya)

  • Diet plays a crucial role in Ayurvedic healing; patients must strictly follow the prescribed Pathya Ahara (diet plan).
  • Meals are prepared hygienically and tailored according to the treatment protocol.
  • Outside food, junk food, and incompatible dietary habits are discouraged during the treatment period.
  • Patients may be advised specific lifestyle routines, including sleep, rest, and activity restrictions.

Patient Responsibilities

Patients are expected to:

  • Provide truthful and complete health information.
  • Follow the prescribed medicines, diet, and therapy schedule diligently
  • Maintain discipline, hygiene, and punctuality for treatments.
  • Treat hospital staff and fellow patients with respect. 
  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and use of unauthorized substances during treatment.

Safety & Hygiene

  • The hospital maintains strict hygiene standards in treatment rooms, patient areas, and kitchen facilities.
  • Infection control and patient safety protocols are followed as per recognized healthcare standards.
  • Patients with infectious symptoms may be referred for appropriate care as per medical guidelines.

Patient Rights

Every patient has the right to:

  • Receive clear information about diagnosis, treatment plan, and expected outcomes.
  • Privacy and confidentiality of medical records. 
  • Safe and respectful care without discrimination.
  • Be informed about treatment costs and duration in advance.

Visitors & Bystanders

  • Overcrowding in patient areas should be avoided to ensure proper rest and recovery.

Personal Conduct

  • Maintain silence and a healing atmosphere within the hospital premises.
  • Use of mobile phones should be limited, especially in treatment areas.
  • Patients are advised not to bring valuables; the hospital is not responsible for loss of personal belongings. 

Discharge & Follow-Up

  • A detailed discharge summary, including medications and diet plan, will be provided.
  • Patients are encouraged to continue follow-up consultations for sustained results.
  • Online or tele-consultation support may be available after discharge.

Commitment to Quality Care

Our hospital adheres to established Ayurvedic healthcare standards focusing on:

  • Patient safety and ethical treatment
  • Transparent communication
  • Continuous quality improvement
  • Holistic healing and long-term wellness
